Pho

A delicious bowl of pho in all its glory

Pho is a Vietnamese soup consisting of bone broth, rice noodles, and thinly sliced meat (usually beef). It may also be served with bean sprouts, fresh herbs, limes, chiles, and other garnishes. The origins of pho are a bit murky, but it is generally believed to have originated in early 20th century northern Vietnam.

Pho usually has a clear beef broth made from bones and meat that's flavored with star anise and cloves for a subtle spice. Rice noodles: These flat noodles are delicate in texture and usually cooked separately to prevent them from getting mushy.

Ingredients

  • Rice Noodles
  • Water
  • Beef broth
  • Star anise
  • Cinnamon
  • Cloves
  • Onion
  • Beef paste
  • Fish Sauce

Steps

  1. Put the beef broth, water, half of the onion in a pot. Bring to boil.
  2. After 10 minutes, put the star anise, cloves, and cinnamon. Let it simmer in the broth for 20 minutes then remove.
  3. Put a spoonful of beef paste and fish sauce in the broth to taste.
  4. To make noodles, place rice noodles in another pot of boiling water. Strain and place in a bowl.
  5. Place toppings such as thinly slice beef, green onion, sliced onions, and beef balls in bowl. Pour broth over top and enjoy!
